TYPES OF ANALYTICS \
> Descriptive \
> Diagnostic \
> Predictive \
> Prescriptive
Descriptive analytics: This form of analytics answers the question, "What happened?" Descriptive analytics focuses on summarizing historical data to understand what has happened or what is happening. This type of analytics provides insights into trends and patterns but does not offer predictions or explanations. \
   Descriptive analytics is often characterized by data visualization, such as pie charts, bar charts, line graphs, tables, or generated narratives. It requires the highest amount of human effort or interpretation. \
Diagnostic analytics: This type involves comparing historical data, often gathered in descriptive analytics, to other data sets to answer the question, \
  “Why did it happen?”. Diagnostic analytics is characterized by techniques, such as drill-down, data discovery, data mining, and correlations. In each of these techniques, multiple data operations and transformations are used for analyzing raw data. This form of analytics provides insights and requires human judgment to turn those insights into actionable information.\
Predictive analytics: This form of analytics answers the question, "What might happen?" This type uses historical data and statistical algorithms to make forecasts about \
  future outcomes. It can help AnyCompany Media anticipate potential scenarios and make informed decisions about how to handle them. Predictive analytics is characterized by \
  techniques, such as machine learning, forecasting, pattern matching, and predictive modeling. For each of these techniques, computers are trained to reverse engineer causality connections in the data.m\
Prescriptive analytics: This technique is based on predictive analytics, but goes one step further and actually recommends actions or responses to the predicted outcome. Prescriptive analytics can analyze \
  the potential implications of different choices and then suggest the best course of action based on those different scenarios. \
  It is characterized by machine learning, graph analysis, simulation, complex event processing, neural networks, and recommendation engines.
